EXAMPLES OF SPORTS

BASKETBALL

a game played between two teams of five players in which goals are scored by throwing a ball through a netted hoop fixed at each end of the court.

SOCCER

a form of football played by two teams of eleven players with a round ball which may not be handled during play except by the goalkeepers.

TENNIS

a game in which two or four players strike a ball with rackets over a net stretched across a court. The usual form (originally called lawn tennis ) is played with a felt-covered hollow rubber ball on a grass, clay, or artificial surface

SWIMMING

swimming, in recreation and sports, the propulsion of the body through water by combined arm and leg motions and the natural flotation of the body.

Sports Rules

these are the rules of the sports listed above;

Rules of Basketball

1.Only five players per team on the court.

2.Score more than your opponent to win.

3.Score within the shot clock.

4.Dribbling advances the ball

5.The offense has five seconds to rebound the ball

6.The offense must advance the ball

7.Ball and ball handler must remain inbounds

8Defenders can't interfere with a shot on a downward trajectory

9.Defenders can legally block or steal the ball

10.Defenders must leave the paint after three seconds

11.Each team is alloted a certain numebr of fouls

12.Illegal contact results in foul

13.Illegal contact results in personal foul

14.Excessive contact results in a flagrant foul

15.Charges and illegal screens result in an offensive foul

Rules of Soccer

1.No hands Unless you're a goal-keeper or attempting a throw-in, youre not allowed to use your hands in play

2.Throw-ins when the ball crosses the sideline going out of bounds a throw-in is taken by a member of the team who didnt kick the ball out

3.Goal Kicks If the offensive team kicks the ball out behind the goal line, the defensive team takes a goal kick from inside the goal box

3.Corner kicks If the defensive team kicks the ball out behind the goal line, the offensive team takes a corner kick from the corner nearest to where the ball left the field

4.Game in play So long as the ball is on the field inside of bounds, the game is considered in play

5.Fouls It's the refree's responsibility to monitor the safe play and fouls, but in general, the intent is ti go for the ball, not the player

Rules of Tennis

. The game starts with a coin toss to determine which player must serve first and which side the want to serve from

. The server must then serve each point from altrnative sides on the base line.

. At no point must the server's feet move in front of the baseline on the court prior to hitting the serve.

. If the server fails to get the first serve in, they may take advantage of the second serve. If they again fail to get their second serve in then a double fault will be called and the point lost.

. If he server clips the net but the ball goes in the service area still then let is called and they get to take the serve againwithout penalty. if the ball hits the net and fails to go in the service area then out is called and they lose that serve

. The receiver may stand where they wish upon the receipt of the serve. If the ball is struck withtout the serve bouncing then the server will receive the point.

. Once a serve has been made, the amount of shots between the players can be unlimited. The point is won by hitting the ball so the opponent fails to return it to the scoring areas.

. Points are awarded in scores of 15,30 and 40. 15 represents 1 point, 30 = 2 and 40 = 3.

. You need 4 points to win a game. If a game land on 40-40, it's known as deuce. From deuce a player needs to win 2 consecutive points to win the game. After winning one point from the deuce, the player is on advantage. If the player wins the next point, they win the game, if they lose it goes back to deuce.

. To win the set a player must win 6 games by 2 or more. The opening sets will go to a tie break if it end up 6-6 where player play first to 7 points. The final set will not have a tie break and requires players to win by two games with no limits.

. If a player touches the net, distracts his opponent or impedes in any way then they automatically lose the point

. The ball can hit any part of the line for the point to be called in, outside the line and the ball is out

. The balls in a tennis match are changed for new balls every 6 games

. A player loses a point if they fail to return the ball in either the correct areas on the court, hits the net and doesn't go into opponent's area or fails to return the ball before it bounces twice in their half

Rules of Swimming

. Swimmers cannot start swimming or moving before the starting signal or buzzer. Doing so will be a false start and the offender will get disqualified

. A disqualification can occur before the starting signal goes off. In this case the starting signal will not get given. The remaining swimmers will get recalled and the race starts again

. As a rule, competitors may not walk during a race. but they can stand (for resting) during a freestyle race or a Freestyle leg of a Medley race

. Each swimmer must make physical contact with the end of the pool of course when turning in all races.

. The turn must get made from the wall and is not permitted to take a stride or step from the bottom of the pool.

. After completing the race swimmers must remain in the water and in their own lane. They must wait in their lane until the refree (or other official authorized by him) releases them.

. The rules if swimming disqualify any swimmer who starts before the signal. The refree observes it and will confirm the starter's observation of the violation.

. The starting signal may sound off before the disqualificationgets declared . In this case the race would continue. The swimmer or swimmers will get disqualified on completion of the race

. No swimmer gets charged with a false start if a recall signal sounds off inadvertently. If a swimmer responds to the 'stand up' command and gets charged with a false start, he may get rleived of the charge.

. The starter must correct a swimmer for any illegal starting position. If not, the swimmer cannot get disqualified.

. Any swimmer may notify the refree 'before the race' of his intent not to compete. In this case he will get disqualified as a declared false start.
